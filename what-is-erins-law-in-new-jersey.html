FAQS

What is Erin's Law?

Erin's Law is a legislation aimed at preventing sexual abuse of children by requiring schools to teach students about personal body safety and how to recognize and report abuse.

When was Erin's Law enacted in New Jersey?

Erin's Law was enacted in New Jersey in 2019, requiring public schools to implement age-appropriate sexual abuse prevention education.

What age group does Erin's Law target in New Jersey schools?

Erin's Law targets students from pre-kindergarten through high school, providing tailored educational materials suitable for each age group.

How can parents access resources related to Erin's Law?

Parents can access resources through school websites, local support organizations, and state education department websites that offer information on sexual abuse prevention programs.

What are some challenges schools face in implementing Erin's Law?

Schools may face challenges such as limited funding, lack of trained staff, and resistance from parents or community members regarding the discussion of sexual abuse in educational settings.
